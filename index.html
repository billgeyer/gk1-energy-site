Financing

Four ways to pay for solar

The right one depends on your goals, your roof, and your bill — this is the general shape of each, not a recommendation. That comes from an actual conversation.

Cash Purchase

You buy the system outright and own it from day one. No interest, no monthly payment — every dollar the system saves is yours, and it's the highest total return over the system's life if you have the capital available.

Solar Loan

You finance the purchase through a bank or solar lender, often with little or nothing down. You still own the system, so once the loan's paid off, the savings are entirely yours too.

Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)

A third party owns, installs, and maintains the system on your property, and you simply buy the power it produces — usually at a lower rate than your utility. $0 upfront, and maintenance isn't your problem.

Solar Lease

Similar to a PPA, but you pay a flat monthly rate for the equipment instead of per kWh. A prepaid version also exists — a discounted lump sum upfront that eliminates the monthly payment entirely.

A note on the federal tax credit: as of 2026, the 30% federal credit for systems you buy with cash or a loan has expired. Third-party-owned systems (lease or PPA) can still pass along a federal credit, since the financing company — not you — owns the equipment. Tax law changes; always confirm current details with your accountant, not this page.
