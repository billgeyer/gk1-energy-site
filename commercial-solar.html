Offices & Operations

Professional & Industrial

Medical, dental, and legal offices, manufacturers, breweries, and dealerships — anywhere electricity is a real, recurring line item and the roof has real surface area to work with.

Open Land

Agricultural

Working farms and farm stands, where a ground-mounted system often makes more sense than a rooftop array, sized around irrigation, cold storage, and equipment loads.

Overnight, Not Residential

Hospitality

Hotels and motels — guests stay overnight, but the building isn't anyone's residence, and usage patterns often make it a strong commercial candidate.

Community

Nonprofits

Churches and other nonprofit organizations — the same large-roof, high-usage test applies, along with nonprofit-specific financing considerations.

Not a current fit

Schools & Government

Excluded by design — the bid/RFP procurement process for this segment isn't part of my current practice, not a reflection of project size or fit.

What it usually delivers

System types and core benefits

System Types

Rooftop: systems on large, flat warehouse or office roofs — using space that's otherwise unused.

Ground-mounted: for properties with open land, like farms or facilities with room to spare.

Solar carports are not part of my current commercial scope.

Cost Savings

Lower monthly utility bills, with predictable energy rates locked in for decades instead of exposure to rising or volatile rates.

Sustainability & ESG

Cuts your organization's carbon footprint, lowers Scope 2 emissions, and helps meet ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) goals — increasingly relevant to lenders, tenants, and stakeholders alike.

Tax Incentives

Businesses can claim the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and use accelerated depreciation to reduce tax bills. Tax specifics are always confirmed with your accountant — never estimated here.
